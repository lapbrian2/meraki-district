Meraki Road did not start as ten districts. It started as a question: what would it look like to build infrastructure worthy of the creators defining this era?

## The problem we kept seeing

The AI-native creative community is extraordinary. The tools are powerful. The practitioners are ambitious. But the infrastructure around them—the credentials, the publishing platforms, the community spaces, the commerce systems—is borrowed from a previous era. Creators are using platforms built for different assumptions, communities optimised for different values, and credentialing systems that measure the wrong things.

## Why ten districts

We could have built one thing. A publishing platform. A community. A credentialing system. But we kept coming back to the same realisation: these problems are interconnected. A credential means nothing without a community that validates it. Publishing means nothing without editorial standards that give it weight. Commerce means nothing without trust built through demonstrated practice.

So we built Meraki Road. Ten districts, each with its own mandate, but all sharing a common infrastructure and a common set of values. Voight Studio handles design and brand strategy. The Road publishes long-form editorial. Meridian translates research into practice. Fieldwork provides structured education. The Provenance maintains the archive. Basecamp hosts the community. Common Ground organises the gatherings. The Mint handles commerce. The Seal manages credentials. The Collective extends the ecosystem through partnerships.

## What comes next

We are building each district to completion, one at a time. The Road launches first—because everything starts with the writing. The essays, the criticism, the dispatches that define what this community thinks and how it communicates. The other districts will follow, each one building on the infrastructure of those that came before.

This is not a startup. It is not a platform. It is a cultural ecosystem built for people who take their practice seriously. Welcome to Meraki Road.
